How the PlayStation 1 Changed Gaming

PlayStation 1 revolutionized the world of gaming with its powerful hardware, immersive 3D graphics, and vast library of diverse titles. It brought gaming to a wider audience and set new standards for entertainment, making it one of the most influential consoles in history.

20 Most Played PS1 Games in Japan

  • バイオハザード (Biohazard) - Resident Evil

    Biohazard / Resident Evil

    Release Date: March 22, 1996

    Overview: A survival horror game where players navigate a mansion overrun by zombies and creatures, uncovering a conspiracy involving bioweapons.

  • ファイナルファンタジーVII (Fainaru Fantajī VII) - Final Fantasy VII

    Final Fantasy VII

    Release Date: January 31, 1997

    Overview: An epic RPG following Cloud Strife and Avalanche as they battle against the corrupt Shinra corporation and Sephiroth, exploring themes of identity and environmentalism.

  • メタルギアソリッド (Metaru Gia Soriddo) - Metal Gear Solid

    Metal Gear Solid

    Release Date: September 3, 1998

    Overview: Stealth action game where Solid Snake infiltrates a nuclear weapons disposal facility to stop terrorists from launching a nuclear strike.

  • ドラゴンクエストVII (Doragon Kuesuto VII) - Dragon Quest VII

    Dragon Quest VII

    Release Date: September 17, 2000

    Overview: An expansive role-playing game where players explore a vast world, uncover secrets, and battle monsters in a classic turn-based system.

  • グランツーリスモ (Guran Tsūrisumo) - Gran Turismo

    Gran Turismo

    Release Date: December 23, 1997

    Overview: A realistic racing simulation featuring a wide variety of cars and tracks, emphasizing driving physics and customization.

  • テイルズ オブ ファンタジア (Teiruzu Obu Fantajia) - Tales of Phantasia

    Tales of Phantasia

    Release Date: December 15, 1995

    Overview: An action RPG with real-time battles, following a group of heroes on a quest to save the world from chaos.

  • パラサイト・イヴ (Parasaito Ivu) - Parasite Eve

    Parasite Eve

    Release Date: March 29, 1998

    Overview: An action RPG blending horror and science fiction, where a police officer faces a biological catastrophe involving mitochondria mutations.

  • クロノ・クロス (Kurono Kurosu) - Chrono Cross

    Chrono Cross

    Release Date: November 18, 1999

    Overview: An RPG sequel to Chrono Trigger, featuring multiple dimensions, a complex story, and a large cast of characters.

  • ファイナルファンタジーVIII (Fainaru Fantajī Hachi) - Final Fantasy VIII

    Final Fantasy VIII

    Release Date: February 11, 1999

    Overview: A story about a group of students turned mercenaries, with a focus on character development, magic, and a complex plot involving time and memory.

  • ドラゴンクエストVIII (Doragon Kuesuto Hachi) - Dragon Quest VIII

    Dragon Quest VIII

    Release Date: November 27, 2004

    Overview: An epic adventure with a prince as the hero, featuring turn-based combat, a vast open world, and charming art style.

  • バイオハザード2 (Biohazard 2) - Resident Evil 2

    Resident Evil 2

    Release Date: January 21, 1999

    Overview: Continues the horror saga with new protagonists, exploring Raccoon City overrun by zombies and bio-weapons.

  • 鉄拳3 (Tekken 3) - Tekken 3

    Tekken 3

    Release Date: March 20, 1998

    Overview: A popular fighting game with diverse characters, fast-paced gameplay, and refined mechanics that set standards for the genre.

  • スーパーロボット大戦α (Super Robot Taisen Alpha) - Super Robot Wars Alpha

    Super Robot Wars Alpha

    Release Date: 2000

    Overview: Tactical RPG featuring mechs from various anime series, with story-driven battles and character interactions.

  • 闘神伝 (Tōshin-den) - Tōshin-den

    Tōshin-den

    Release Date: 1997

    Overview: Fighting game with unique characters and gameplay mechanics, popular among niche audiences.

  • ミスタードリラー (Misutā Dorirā) - Mr. Driller

    Mr. Driller

    Release Date: 1999

    Overview: Puzzle game where players drill through colorful blocks, avoiding falling debris and reaching depths.

  • ペプシマン (Pepsi Man) - Pepsi Man

    Pepsi Man

    Release Date: 1999

    Overview: An endless runner game featuring Pepsi's mascot, running through city streets avoiding obstacles while collecting soda cans.

About Luís Otávio Dias de Oliveira

Hello! My name is Luís Otávio Dias de Oliveira, and I am passionate about video games, particularly retro games from the 90s and early 2000s. Ever since I was a child, I have been fascinated by the evolution of gaming technology and storytelling, which has led me to develop a deep appreciation for classic consoles like the PlayStation 1 and PSP.

Growing up in Brazil, I spent countless hours playing, exploring, and analyzing games that shaped my childhood and influenced my aspirations. My favorite genres include RPGs, adventure games, and fighting games, all of which have contributed to my understanding of game design and narrative development.

Throughout my journey, I have also developed an interest in game preservation and the history of gaming. I believe that understanding the roots of modern gaming helps us appreciate the artistry and innovation behind each title. This passion has motivated me to create this website, which serves both as a personal project and as a tribute to the games and consoles I love.

Apart from gaming, I enjoy programming, exploring new technologies, and learning about digital art and design. I am always eager to expand my skills and collaborate with others who share similar interests.
